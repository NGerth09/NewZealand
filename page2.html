Legal age to vote in elections, drink alcohol, and drive a car.

The legal age to vote in elections in New Zealand is 18, stated in 1974.
The legal age to drink alcohol in New Zealand is 18 as well, people must provide their ID in order to buy and drink alcohol.
The legal age to drive a car in New Zealand used to be at 15 years old but 9 years ago the government decided to increase it to 16 years old.
*There is a special alcohol limit if you’re driving = For drivers under 20 years old the limit is zero alcohol consumption. The alcohol limit for drivers aged 20 years and over is 250 micrograms of alcohol per litre of breath and the blood alcohol limit is 50 milligrams*

Traditions/celebrations unique to this country/culture.

New Zealand is a beautiful place filles of unique culture and traditions, one of them that is probably well known in sports around the world is the haka, it’s a famous traditional Maori war dance, has been immortalised by New Zealand’s All Blacks team, this dance is done to incite fear their opponents and proclaim the strength of the Maori fighters. Another tradition is the hongi, not the hangi, the hangi is a traditional meal, the hongi is a special greeting saved for special occasions where the two participants touch foreheads and noses and it symbolises the passing of the breath of life from one person to another.
