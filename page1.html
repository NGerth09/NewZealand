Geography

Country Name, Flag, Map, neighboring geography, and capital

New Zealand is an island country (that encloses two main islands and other groups of smaller islands) that belongs to the Australian continent. It is located in the southeast part of the globe, in the South Pacific Ocean. Its closest neighbour is Australia. New Zealand's capital is Wellington. Their flag has symbolic value. The navy blue on the background represents the blue ocean and the clear sky. The Southern Cross Constellation refers to their location in the South Pacific Ocean, and the UK flag recognizes their historical origins as a British Colony.

Number of States or Regions

New Zealand is divided into 16 regions, Northland, Auckland, Waikato, Bay of Plenty, Gisborne, Hawke's Bay, Taranaki, Manawatu-Wanganui, Wellington, Tasman/Nelson, Marlborough, West Coast, Canterbury, Otago and Southland.

Politics and Currency

Type of Political System and current Leaders

New Zealand is a constitutional monarchy with a parliamentary system of government. This means that the head of state is a sovereign (currently Queen Elizabeth II).
- The Queen is represented in NZ by the Governor-General, Dame Patsy Reddy. (Constitutional functions of the governor-general include presiding over the Executive Council, appointing ministers and judges, granting royal assent to legislation, and summoning and dissolving parliament.)
- The position of Prime Minister of New Zealand is currently held by Jacinda Ardern. (The prime minister chairs the Cabinet and selects its members, advises the Crown on the exercise of executive power, and acts as the head of government for New Zealand.)
- The Government is formed after a democratic election held every 3 years.
